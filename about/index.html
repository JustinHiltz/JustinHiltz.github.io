About myself

I am a web developer from Toronto with a focus on Ruby on Rails. I have front-end development experience utilizing JavaScript, AngularJS, HTML5, CSS3/Sass/SCSS, Jekyll, and Foundation. I follow test-driven development (TDD) practices using RSpec and minitest. I have some experience developing with PHP as well.

I am consistently trying to learn and improve my programming skills. I have completed the Ruby, JavaScript, AngularJS, HTML & CSS, and PHP tracks from Codecademy, the excellent Rails Tutorial by Michael Hartl, keep sharp by solving problems on Codewars.

Outside of work you will find me riding my bike all over Toronto. I especially enjoy riding the Martin Goodman Trail along the waterfront and the Don River Trail on the east end. There are a lot of great craft breweries in Toronto that I like to visit to pick something up to enjoy when I get home. Every summer I get out to some of my favourite provincial parks for camping and canoe trips.

I listen to a lot of podcasts.

Here are some of my favourites:

Debug with Guy English and Rene Ritchie is a podcast on development mainly for Mac OS and iOS that features prominent developers in casual conversation.

Accidental Tech Podcast with Marco Arment, John Siracusa, and Casey Liss is a tech podcast that mainly focuses on the Apple world but does a good job of discussing tech as a whole and the controversies of the week.

Roderick on the Line with Merlin Mann and John Roderick is podcast that is just a conversation between two friends with great stories.

Caustic Soda with Toren Atkinson, Kevin Leeson, and Joe Fulgham is a podcast about the science, history, and pop culture of the weird, gross and disturbing.
